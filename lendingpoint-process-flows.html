What is This Process?

Understanding the daily LendingPoint operations workflow

The LendingPoint process encompasses two parallel workflows that handle loan operations for Coastal Community Bank:

üîÑ Program Loans

Loans originated and serviced by LendingPoint where CCB is the bank of record. Daily files track disbursements, payments, and charge-offs.

GL Accounts: 10226000, 10215000, 10426000, 10415000

üì¶ Purchased Loans

Loans purchased from LendingPoint that CCB now owns outright. Separate tracking with different GL accounts and subledgers.

GL Accounts: 10226050, 10215050, 10426050, 10415050

üí± Settlement Accounts

Bank control accounts that receive wires and process payments. Must reconcile to zero daily after all entries.

Repayments: 1600020521 | ACH: 1600020497

üìã ATE Templates

Accounting Template Entries that translate payment data into journal entries. Generated by Excel parse tools daily.

Parse ‚Üí Validate ‚Üí ATE Template ‚Üí Upload

Key Concepts to Understand

Essential terminology and concepts used throughout the processes

üìä Day Tapes

Day1_Tape: Yesterday's loan-level snapshot
Day2_Tape: Today's snapshot. Comparing these reveals what changed (new loans, payments, charge-offs).

üî¢ Transaction Prefixes

LPT-: Loan Payment Transaction
RTA-: Return Transaction (reversals)

üì¶ Product Types

‚è±Ô∏è DPD Thresholds

üí∞ Excess Payments

When borrower pays more than owed. Held in settlement account until refunded. Tracked in Excess_Filter sheet.

üìè OOB (Out of Balance)

Penny rounding differences where Principal + Interest ‚â† Total Payment. Max $0.09/payment, auto-adjusted.

Excess Payments & Refunds

Handling overpayments that exceed loan balance

Excess Payment Received

When borrower pays more than loan balance, the excess is included in the wire but NOT applied to principal/interest. It remains in the Settlement Account.

Excess_Filter: =FILTER(Pymt_File!$A:$Y, (Pymt_File!$E:$E=Parse!$C$2)*(Pymt_File!$J:$J<>0),"")

Refund Issued

Refunds reduce the wire amount. ADJ- transactions with Adjustment_Type = "Refund" create negative excess entries.

Transaction Code: 60 for refunds (negative excess), 30 for overpayments (positive)

OOB Adjustments

Penny rounding differences where P + I ‚â† Total. Max $0.09 per payment. Auto-captured in OOB_Filter.

OOB_Filter: =FILTER(Pymt_File!$A:$AA, Pymt_File!$AA:$AA<>0)

Active Loan

The loan is current or has minor delinquency (< 120 DPD). Payments are processed normally through the Parse sheet. Principal, interest, and status are tracked daily in the Day Tapes.

When Days_Past_Due (Column K) exceeds 119, the loan enters CCB's internal charge-off tracking. This is BEFORE LendingPoint officially charges it off.

Critical Check: Credits = Debits

This MUST always pass. If it fails, the journal is unbalanced and cannot be posted. Every dollar debited must equal every dollar credited.

Wire Variance Handling

A -$0.02 variance is typically OOB (penny rounding). Auto-captured in OOB_Filter and adjusted via P35:P40 sums. Larger variances require investigation.
